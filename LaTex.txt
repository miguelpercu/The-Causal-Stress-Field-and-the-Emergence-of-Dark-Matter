\begin{abstract}
The phenomenon of Dark Matter (DM) in galaxies and clusters is addressed by the **Unified Causal Principle (UCP)** as a **geometric correction** required for \textbf{Causal Homeostasis}, rather than a non-baryonic particle. This mechanism is governed by the **Causal Coherence Constant ($\mathbf{\kappa_{crit}}$) which sets the acceleration threshold ($\mathbf{A_{UCP}}$) below which the spacetime must distort its local geometry to avoid a catastrophic causal collapse, exemplified by the gravitational singularity of a black hole. We derive the Causal Interpolation Function ($\mu_{\mathbf{UCP}}$) from first principles, successfully predicting the observed flat galactic rotation curves. Numerical results confirm that the velocity difference attributed to DM is entirely accounted for by the UCP's correction factor, establishing MOC as a thermodynamically mandated consequence of the causal structure of the universe.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction: The Causal Problem of Gravitational Collapse}

The standard cosmological model ($\Lambda$CDM) requires the existence of a non-baryonic component, Dark Matter (DM), to explain the observed rotation speeds of galaxies. This paper applies the \textbf{Unified Causal Principle (UCP)} framework, which has already provided a quantifiable solution to the Hubble Tension \cite{Percudani_UCP_Hubble} and the quantum measurement problem \cite{Percudani_UCP_QM}, to this final major enigma.

The UCP asserts that the universe operates under a strict \textbf{Thermodynamic Causal Constraint} to maintain a global entropic balance ($\dot{S}_{\text{net}} \approx 0$). In this context, the existence of DM is the necessary defense mechanism against the gravitational conditions that lead to a \textbf{Causal Singularity}. The collapse into an **Agujero Negro** is the point where the Causal Homeostasis fails entirely \cite{Percudani_UCP_Note}.

\section{Derivation of the Causal Correction Factor}
\label{sec:derivation}

The effective acceleration ($a_{obs}$) is the result of the Newtonian acceleration ($a_N$) corrected by the Causal Interpolation Function ($\mu_{\mathbf{UCP}}$).

\subsection{The Causal Interpolation Function ($\mu_{\mathbf{UCP}}$)}

The effective acceleration is defined by:
\begin{equation}
    a_{obs} = \frac{a_N}{\mu_{\mathbf{UCP}}}
    \label{eq:a_obs_def}
\end{equation}

To enforce Causal Homeostasis, the UCP derives the Interpolation Function required to ensure $a_{obs}$ stabilizes at $\mathbf{A_{UCP}}$ (ec. \ref{eq:a_ucp}) when $a_N$ is small:
\begin{equation}
    \mu_{\mathbf{UCP}} = \frac{a_N}{a_N + \mathbf{A_{UCP}}}
    \label{eq:mu_ucp}
\end{equation}
The function $\mu_{\mathbf{UCP}}$ represents the strength of the DM effect.

\subsection{The Emergence of Flat Rotation Curves}

Substituting the UCP function (ec. \ref{eq:mu_ucp}) into the effective acceleration (ec. \ref{eq:a_obs_def}) yields the final UCP law of gravity at galactic scales:
\begin{equation}
    a_{obs} = a_N \left( \frac{a_N + \mathbf{A_{UCP}}}{a_N} \right) = a_N + \mathbf{A_{UCP}}
    \label{eq:a_obs_final}
\end{equation}

Equation \ref{eq:a_obs_final} demonstrates that in the exterior of a galaxy, where $a_N \ll \mathbf{A_{UCP}}$, the observed acceleration is effectively $a_{obs} \approx \mathbf{A_{UCP}}$. This constant floor in acceleration is the physical cause of the **flat rotation curves**.
